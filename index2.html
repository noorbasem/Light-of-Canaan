Palestinian Heritage

Palestinian Embroidery (Tatreez)
A traditional hand-stitched art that tells stories through colorful patterns, unique to each village.
Dabke Dance
A vibrant folk dance performed during celebrations, symbolizing unity and joy.
Palestinian Thobe
The traditional embroidered women's dress represents the cultural and social identity of Palestinian women.
Palestinian keffiyeh
The iconic black-and-white scarf symbolizing Palestinian identity and resistance.
Olive Wood Carving
A handicraft that began in Bethlehem during the Byzantine era, where olive wood is carved into refined art that continues to this day.
Hebron glass
Traditional handmade glass making in Hebron has its roots in Roman times, and its secrets are kept preserved among artisan families.
Hikaye
A traditional narrative style recounted by women in home gatherings, expressing the values and attitudes of Palestinian society.
Henna
Palestinian henna is a traditional art form used in celebrations, especially weddings, where intricate designs are applied to the hands and feet symbolizing joy, beauty, and cultural heritage.
Music of Palestine
The Music of Palestine reflects the rich cultural heritage of the Palestinian people, blending traditional folk melodies with contemporary influences
Darbuka
The darbuka is a traditional hand drum widely used in Palestinian and Middle Eastern music, providing lively rhythms for weddings and celebrations.
Palestinian art
Art expressing Palestinian identity and struggle through painting, sculpture, and graffiti, conveying messages of freedom and homeland.
Jerusalem Markets
The old markets gather vendors and artisans in historic alleys, offering fruits, spices, and traditional crafts in a lively atmosphere.
Olive Trees
The olive tree is a symbol of peace and resilience in Palestine, and it is grown in most Palestinian lands.
Traditional Palestinian Cuisine
Distinctive Palestinian dishes that reflect the richness of traditional Arab cuisine and are served on special occasions.
Handmade pottery
traditional earthenwareHandmade vessels that reflect the arts of ancient Palestinian craftsmanship.
Wood carving
A traditional art that reflects the skill of Palestinians in making handicrafts.
Islamic calligraphy
Arabic calligraphy and ornamentation is an ancient art form that adorns religious manuscripts, books, and mosques, expressing spiritual and cultural beauty.
Architecture of Palestine
The traditional Palestinian architecture is characterized by unique designs that reflect the traditions of the inhabitants and their daily lifestyles, with the use of local materials and ancient building techniques that preserve architectural heritage.
Golden Markets
Markets specializing in traditional and modern gold jewelry, featuring unique Palestinian designs.
Al-Zawiya Market
A historic and popular market in Gaza City, known for traditional shops selling fabrics, clothing, and handicrafts, reflecting Gaza’s authentic heritage.
Arabic coffee
Arabic coffee in Palestine is more than a drink; it’s a social ritual that brings people together. Made from freshly roasted beans, it’s served in small cups with dates, accompanied by conversation and hospitality as a sign of welcome.
